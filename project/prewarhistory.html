Pre-War History

"You have a row of dominoes set up; you knock over the first one, and what will happen to the last one is that it will go over very quickly."
- President Dwight Eisenhower
Colonial French forces driving away the local Vietnamese

 After the unification of the Nguyen dynasty in 19th, there was an effor made by the emperor to expel French Catholic missionaries from Vietnam. The French responded when Napoleon the Third issued an order to attack the imperial port of Đà Nẵng. Over time, the French forced the Vietnamese to cede a majority of their land as French territory.

 Tensions rose for almost a century, coming to a conclusion in the massive First Indochina War from 1946 to 1954. This was a war with a northern communist resistance force backed by China and the USSR against the imperialist French government. In the end, the French were driven almost completelyout of Indochina. However, the newly independent North Vietnam adopted a socialist ideology and refused to join with the south. The two conflicting countries and ideologies would clash for another few decades, finally culminating in the Vietnam War.

A brief timeline of events:

  • 1858: The port of Đà Nẵng fallls to French troops led by French general Charles Rigault de Genouilly.
  • 1930: Communist movements begin to take hold in French Vietnam.
  • 1940: Japan invades Vietnam and installs a puppet government.
  • 1945: Hồ Chí Minh declares Vietnam independence with a socialist government.
  • 1946: France declares war on the newly independent country, beginning the First Indochina War.
